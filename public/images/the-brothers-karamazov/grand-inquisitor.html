The Grand Inquisitor — Ivan's Parable
📍 The Scene
Christ returns to 16th-century Seville during the Inquisition. The people recognise him and he performs miracles. The Grand Inquisitor has him arrested and visits him in his cell at night.
⚖️ The Inquisitor's Case
"Mankind cannot bear freedom. We corrected your work. We gave them miracle, mystery, and authority. They are happy — as long as they obey us. You gave them freedom; we gave them bread."
🕊️ Christ's Answer
Christ says nothing. He kisses the old man gently on the lips. The Inquisitor is shaken but releases him, saying: "Go, and come no more."
The parable asks: Is freedom a gift or a burden? Can humanity handle truth without authority?
